## Phase 2: Where to Find Jobs

### Best Job Boards for AI Engineers
| Platform | Focus | Why It's Good |
|----------|-------|---------------|
| **LinkedIn** | Mainstream | Most companies post here, good filters for "AI Engineer" |
| **Wellfound (AngelList)** | Startups | Equity-heavy, fast-moving, more forgiving of career switches |
| **Y Combinator Jobs** | YC Startups | Often hiring generalists, "AI" is hot in YC |
| **Remote OK / We Work Remotely** | Remote-first | Global opportunities, often more flexible on background |
| **HackerNews Who's Hiring** | Tech community | Monthly thread with direct founder contact |
| **AI-specific boards** | Niche AI roles | - **AI Jobs Board** (aijobs.net)<br>- **MLOps Jobs** (mlops.community)<br>- **HuggingFace Jobs** |

### Company Target List (Build Your Own)

**Tier 1: AI-First Companies**
- OpenAI, Anthropic, Cohere (infra/API/tools teams)
- Pinecone, Qdrant, Weaviate (vector DB companies)
- LangChain, LlamaIndex (tooling companies)
- Perplexity, Character.AI (AI product companies)

**Tier 2: Companies Building AI Products**
- Notion, Slack, GitHub (adding AI features to existing products)
- Startups building vertical AI solutions (legal AI, medical AI, etc.)
- AI consulting/services firms

**Tier 3: Traditional Tech Adding AI**
- Meta, Google, Microsoft (large teams, more competitive)
- SaaS companies adding "AI features"
- Your local tech companies (less competition geographically)

**How to prioritize:**
- Are they **building** AI or just **using** AI? (You want builders)
- Do they mention "production AI", "AI infrastructure", "LLM platforms"? (Good signals)
- Check their engineering blog—are they writing about RAG, embeddings, vector search? (Great sign)

## Phase 3: Networking (Warm Intros > Cold Apply)

### Strategy: Warm Intro → Coffee Chat → Referral → Interview

**Where to network:**

1. **Twitter (X)**
   - Follow: @simonw, @swyx, @llama_index, @LangChainAI, @OpenAI
   - Engage: Reply to their tweets with thoughtful comments (not spam)
   - Post: Share what you're building ("Built a RAG system that does X, here's what I learned")
   - Target 2-3 tweets/week

2. **LinkedIn**
   - Connect with AI engineers at target companies
   - Message template:
     ```
     Hi [Name],

     I saw your post about [specific thing they shared] and really resonated with [why].

     I'm transitioning from 8 years of full-stack engineering into AI (building RAG systems, LLM integrations). Would love to learn about your journey into AI engineering at [Company].

     Happy to buy you a virtual coffee if you have 15-20 minutes!

     Best,
     [Your Name]
     ```
   - 20% of people will respond. That's all you need.

3. **Local Meetups**
   - Search Meetup.com for: "Machine Learning", "AI Engineers", "Python", "Data Science"
   - **Show up consistently** (3+ times)—you'll start recognizing people
   - Don't pitch yourself. Ask questions: "What are you working on?" "What AI stack do you use?"
   - **Follow up on LinkedIn** after meeting

4. **Discord / Slack Communities**
   - LangChain Discord
   - Qdrant Community
   - r/MachineLearning Discord
   - LocalLLaMA subreddit/Discord
   - **Be helpful:** Answer questions, share your projects, don't just lurk

5. **Open Source Contributions**
   - Pick a tool you use: sentence-transformers, langchain, qdrant-client
   - Fix small bugs, improve docs, add examples
   - Maintainers notice—some will offer to connect

## Phase 4: The Application Strategy

### Resume Targeting
**You need 2-3 versions of your resume:**

1. **"Full-Stack AI Engineer"** (balanced)
   - Lead with AI projects, but show frontend/backend balance
   - Target: Companies wanting full-stack engineers who can build AI features

2. **"Applied AI Engineer"** (AI-heavy)
   - AI projects first, frontend experience compressed to 1 section
   - Target: AI-first companies, LLM platforms, AI infrastructure

3. **"Backend + AI"** (backend-heavy)
   - Python, Flask, APIs, databases prominent
   - AI as "plus" skill
   - Target: Backend roles at companies adding AI features

### Cold Application Strategy (When no referral)

**Don't just click "Easy Apply". Do this:**

1. **Research the company** (15 minutes)
   - Read their engineering blog
   - Check their tech stack (job posting, StackShare, LinkedIn)
   - Find something specific to mention

2. **Customize your resume**
   - Mirror their job posting keywords
   - If they say "RAG experience required", make sure "RAG" appears 3x in your resume
   - Quantify: "Built RAG system processing 10K documents"

3. **Write a short cover letter** (3 paragraphs, 150 words)
   - **P1**: I'm applying for [role] because [specific reason about their company]
   - **P2**: I bring [X years] of engineering + [specific AI skills you built]. I've built [specific project] that does [specific thing their company cares about].
   - **P3**: I'd love to discuss how my background in [X] can help [Company] with [specific challenge]. [Attach resume]

4. **Follow up with hiring manager on LinkedIn** (2 days later)
   - Find the hiring manager (engineering manager, director of AI)
   - Send a **short** note:
     ```
     Hi [Name],

     I applied for the [Role] at [Company] and wanted to personally express my interest.

     I've been building RAG pipelines and LLM integrations, most recently [specific project]. I'm excited about [specific thing about their company/product].

     I'd love to chat about how my experience can contribute to [team/project].

     [Link to GitHub project]

     Best,
     [Name]
     ```

## Phase 5: The Numbers Game

**Reality check:**
- 100 applications → 10 responses → 3 phone screens → 1-2 onsites → 1 offer
- **With referrals:** 10 targeted applications → 5 responses → 3 screens → 2 onsites → 1 offer

**Weekly targets:**
- 5-10 applications (quality > quantity)
- 3-5 networking messages (LinkedIn, Twitter, email)

## Phase 6: When You Get the Interview

### Prep Checklist (1 week before)
- [ ] Review their engineering blog (know their stack)
- [ ] Practice system design (design a system similar to their product)
- [ ] Review your own projects (be ready to deep-dive for 20 min)
- [ ] Prepare 5 questions to ask them
- [ ] Do a mock interview with a friend
- [ ] Test your internet, camera, background for video call

### During the Interview
- **Take notes** when they explain the role/tech
- **Ask clarifying questions** during coding/system design
- **Think out loud** (they want to see your process)
- **Be honest about gaps** ("I haven't used X in production, but here's how I'd approach learning it")

### After the Interview
- Send thank-you email **same day** (2-3 sentences, mention something specific from the call)
- If you don't hear back in 1 week, follow up politely

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Applying only to "senior" AI roles** → Apply to "mid-level" and "full-stack" roles too  
❌ **Resume says "learning AI"** → Resume says "building AI systems"  
❌ **Only applying online** → Networking has 5x better conversion  
❌ **Generic applications** → Customized, researched applications  
❌ **Not following up** → Persistence (politely) shows interest  
❌ **Waiting until "ready"** → You're ready now (interviews are learning experiences)

## Key Takeaways
1. **GitHub + LinkedIn** are your storefront—make them shine
2. **Networking > Cold applications** (5x better conversion)
3. **Be specific** in your pitch (not "I like AI", but "I build RAG systems")
4. **Quantity + Quality**: 5-10 targeted applications/week
5. **Follow up** (most people don't—this is your edge)
6. **You're interviewing them too**—find the right fit
